Mohammad Hatta

1st Vice President of Indonesia

Mohammad Hatta in 1950

    Here's a time line of Mohammad Hatta life:


  • 12 August 1902 born in Fort De Kock (now known as Bukittinggi)
  • 1913 - 1916 He went to the Dutch language elementary school (ELS or Europeesche Lagere School) in Padang after he had finished Sekolah Melayu ('Malay School') in Bukittinggi.
  • 1916-1919 Treasurer of Jong Sumatranen Bond in Padang
  • 1920 - 1921 Treasurer of Jong Sumatranen Bond in Jakarta and take care of magazines Jong Sumatra
  • 1922 - 1925 Being a member Indonesische Vereniging (when studying in the Netherlands) which later turned into Perhimpoenan Indonesia, and became Editorial Board for magazine of independent Indonesia
  • 1925 - 1930 Chairman of the Indonesian Youth in the Netherlands
  • 1927 - 1928 Arrested and imprisoned in Den Haag, Netherlands (from 23 September 1927 to 22 March 1928) because of his writings in the magazine Indonesia Merdeka
  • 1927 - 1931 As representatives of Indonesia in the movement of the League Against Imperialism and Colonialism, based in Berlin
  • 1936 Join the IV Demokratique International Congress in Beirvile, Paris
  • 1932 back to Indonesia
  • 1934 - 1935 hairman of the Party of National Education of Indonesia (commonly known as the new PNI) and handle the magazine Daulat Rakyat
  • 1934 Dutch government imprisoned in Glodok, Jakarta
  • 1934 - 1935 Discarded to Boven Digul, Papua
  • 1935 - 1942 Discarded to Banda Naira
  • February 1942 Moved to Prison in Sukabumi
  • March 9, 1942 Released from prison
  • April 1942 Head Office of Counsel to the government troops Dai Nippon
  • 1943 Appointed as one of the central leadership of the People's Power (Putera)
  • May 1945 Members Dokuritzu Zyunbi Tyoosakai (Investigation Agency of Efforts Independence)
  • 7 August 1945 Vice Chairman of the Preparatory Committee for Indonesian Independence (PPKI)
  • 17 August 1945 Proclaiming Independence with Soekarno
  • 18 August 1945 to 1 December 1956 Vice President of Indonesia I
  • October 16, 1945 Removing the edict No. X which provides power to determine the Broad Outlines of State Policy to the Central Indonesian National Committee (KNIP)
  • 1 November 1945 Removing Notices Politics which among others stated that Indonesia was willing to settle the dispute with the Netherlands by way of diplomacy
  • 3 November 1945 Removing Notices which opens opportunities for the establishment of political parties
  • January 29, 1948 to December 1949 Vice President and concurrently as Prime Minister and Minister of Defense
  • 1949 hairman of the Indonesian delegation to the Round Table Conference in Den Haag and accept the transfer of sovereignty from the Queen Juliana
  • December 1949 to August 1950 Vice President and concurrently as Prime Minister and Foreign Minister in the RIS Cabinet OTHER ACTIVITIES
  • 1951 - 1961 Being a lecturer at the Army Staff College in Bandung
  • 1954 - 1959 Being a lecturer at Gadjah Mada University in Yogyakarta
  • 1966 - 1971 Outstanding Lecturer at the University of Hasanuddin
  • 1969 Advisor to the President and the Advisory Commission on the issue of corruption
  • 1967 - 1971 Outstanding Lecturer at the Padjadjaran University, Bandung
  • 1975 Chairman of the Committee of Five which will provide the formulation of the interpretation of Pancasila
  • 14 March 1980 Hatta died in Jakarta and was buried in Jakarta's Tanah Kusir public cemetery. He was declared a "Proclamation Hero" by the Suharto government in 1986
